Help about: Claiming Gift Aid

Gift Aid is a repayment of tax on donations from individuals who have made a valid Gift Aid declaration.

You can make a Gift Aid claim on:

  • single donations
  • regular donations from an individual donor
  • donations of £20 or less from different donors that you have added together (aggregated donations)
  • donations raised through sponsored events

If you select ‘Yes’, you need to complete a Gift Aid spreadsheet, and attach it to the ‘Attach Gift Aid spreadsheet’ page.

Help about: Other income claims

You can claim on donations from individuals, with special rules for what you can and cannot claim on. Apart from individual donations, you can also claim a repayment of tax deducted from other income, for example:

  • bank and building society interest
  • interest from government stocks
  • income from wayleaves, for example, sums paid by a utility company for the right to put cables across a piece of land
  • royalties
  • other types of annual payment
  • estate income
  • discretionary trust income

If you select ‘Yes’, you need to complete a other income spreadsheet, and attach it to the ‘Attach other income spreadsheet’ page.

The GASDS is for small donations of £30 or less that the charity or community amateur sports club (CASC) has received that you do not have a Gift Aid declaration for.
Help about: Gift Aid Small Donations Scheme claims

To be eligible to make a claim under GASDS, your charity or CASC must have claimed Gift Aid:

  • in the same tax year as you want to claim GASDS
  • without getting a Gift Aid or GASDS claim penalty in the last 2 tax years

You must claim for top-up payments under the GASDS within 2 years of the end of the tax year that the donations were collected in.

For GASDS claims, you must claim by tax year, not based on your accounting periods.

GASDS donations can be in:

  • cash
  • contactless card payments you collected on or after 6 April 2019

You can claim up to 25% of GASDS donations. The maximum donation total you can submit a claim for under the GASDS in one tax year is £8,000. Your charity or CASC would recieve a payment of £2,000 from a maximum £8,000 GASDS claim.

Guidance is available on the amount of GASDS top-up payment you can claim.

Help about: GASDS donations

You can make a claim under the GASDS on donations of £30 or less that were not collected in a community building.

This is for donations within 2 years of the end of the tax year that they were collected in.

Help about: GASDS community buildings

You can claim on GASDS donations of £30 or less that were collected in a community building.

This is for donations within 2 years of the end of the tax year that they were collected in.

CASCs cannot claim under the community buildings rules.

If you select ‘Yes’, you need to complete a community building spreadsheet, and attach it to the ‘Attach community buildings spreadsheet’ page.

Help about: Connected charities

Charities and CASCs are generally connected under GASDS rules where one or more of the following applies:

  • one charity controls another
  • two or more charities are under common control
  • control links a number of charities - for example, if Charity A controls Charity B and Charity B controls Charity C, then all 3 charities are connected

If connected charities or CASCs have different purposes or activities, then they will not be considered a connected charity under GASDS rules.

The connected charities and CASCs must share the maximum GASDS donation claim limit of £8,000.

If you select ‘Yes’, you need to complete a connected charities spreadsheet, and attach it to the ‘Attach connected charities spreadsheet’ page.

ERROR: Select if you are adjusting a previous GASDS claim.

Help about: Adjustment to previous GASDS claim

Some claims can have errors in which a charity or CASC receives more than their entitled amount for a GASDS claim.

If this is the case, the tax overclaimed can be inputted in the ‘Previously over-claimed amount’ box of a spreadsheet. Make sure to enter the amount of tax overclaimed and not the total value of the donations.

If the overpaid amount is less than the current repayment claim, it will be deducted from the total amount of this claim.

If the overpaid amount is more than the repayment claim you are making, then you will need to make an additional payment to HMRC.
