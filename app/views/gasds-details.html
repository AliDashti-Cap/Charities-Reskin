Gift Aid Small Donations Scheme (GASDS) details

You have selected to claim a repayment of tax on GASDS donations. This is separate from your Gift Aid claim.

If you do not want to complete this type of claim, you can remove it by going to the Claim details page and answering ‘No’ to either:

  • ‘Are you claiming a top up payment under GASDS for donations that were not collected in a community building?’
  • ‘Are you claiming a top up payment for small cash donations under Gift Aid Small Donations Scheme (GASDS)?’

The GASDS is for small donations of £30 or less that the charity or community amateur sports club (CASC) has received that you do not have a Gift Aid declaration for. It only applies to donations you have received within 2 years of the end of the tax year that the donations were collected in.

Is my tax year eligible under GASDS?

GASDS only applies to donations you have received within 2 years of the end of the tax year that the donations were collected in.

For example, if you received a donation on January 1 2020, the end of the tax year is April 5 2020. This means you need to claim this donation by April 5 2022.

What amount of donations can I claim under GASDS?

You can claim up to 25% of GASDS donations. The maximum donation total you can submit a claim for under the GASDS in one tax year is £8,000. Your charity or CASC would recieve a payment of £2,000 from a maximum £8,000 GASDS claim.

Help about: Top up payment over claimed

Some claims can have errors in which a charity or CASC receives more than their entitled amount for a GASDS claim. If this is the case, the tax overclaimed can be inputted in this field.

If the overpaid amount is less than the current repayment claim, it will be deducted from the total amount of this claim.

If the overpaid amount is more than the repayment claim you are making, then you will need to make an additional payment to HMRC.
