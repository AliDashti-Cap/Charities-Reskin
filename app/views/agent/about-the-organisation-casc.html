Help about: Who should HMRC send the payment to?

Payment to the agent or nominee

The repayment will be paid, via Bacs (Bankers Automated Clearing System), into the account that HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) currently has on record for the charity or CASC you are claiming on behalf of. If the bank account details have changed then the charity or CASC must tell HMRC about their new account before you make this claim. They must complete the change of details form at least 30 days before this claim is made. If they do not, this could delay the payment or could result in the payment going into the wrong bank account.

Payment to the charity or CASC

A payment can only be made by Bacs into the account of an agent or nominee if the charity has authorised HMRC to do this. If not then the payment will be made into the charity's or CASC's bank account.
