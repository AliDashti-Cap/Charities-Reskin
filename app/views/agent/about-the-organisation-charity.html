Help about: Who should the payment be sent to?

Payment to a charity or CASC

HMRC will make the payment via Bacs into the account that they currently have on record for the organisation you are making the claim for.

If the bank account details of an organisation are not the same as the details in HMRC records, the organisation must update the details before you make the claim. An organisation can do this by submitting a change of details form (ChV1) at least 30 days before you make the claim.

If bank account details are not up to date, this could delay the payment or result in the payment going into the wrong bank account.

Payment to an agent or nominee

HMRC can only make a payment into the account of an agent or nominee if the organisation has authorised it.

If not, then the payment will be made into the organisations bank account.

Who is eligible for no registration with a charity regulator?

You are excepted if income is £100,000 or less and your charity is in one of the following groups:

  • churches or chapels belonging to some Christian denominations
  • charities that provide premises for some types of school
  • Scout and Guide groups
  • charitable service funds of the armed forces
  • student unions

You are exempt from registering with the relevant regulator because you are:

  • an educational institution such as a university, school or connected institution
  • a museum or gallery, as defined in the Charities Act
  • a housing association registered under the Co-operative and Community Benefit Societies Act (CCBS) and/or the CU Act 1965

You can apply directly to HMRC for recognition as a charity for tax purposes, if you are:

  • excepted from registration with a charity regulator
  • exempt from registration with a charity regulator
  • your organisation is established in a country where there is no charity regulator
