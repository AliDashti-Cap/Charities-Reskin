Organisation details

To make this claim, you must be a recognised authorised official or corporate trustee that has already been notified to HMRC as the authorised official.

You need to provide details about the charity or Community Amateur Sports Club (CASC) you are claiming on behalf of.

Help about: Charity regulator

Community Amateur Sports Clubs (CASCs) are not regulated by an external organisation. Therefore, CASCs should always select 'None' from the drop-down list.

ERROR: Enter the charity registration number for your organisation

Enter the numerical characters only of the registration number the charity regulator issued to you. This is not the HMRC charities reference number.
Help about: Charity registration number

The registration number from the regulator will be on any paperwork received from that regulator.

If the charity is dual registered, you can either:

  • include a registration number used in previous correspondence with HMRC
  • complete and submit a change of details form (ChV1) with this information before making your claim.

ERROR: Select a reason for not being registered with a regulator

Help about: No registration with regulator

You can apply directly to HMRC for recognition as a charity for tax purposes, if you are:

  • excepted from registration with a charity regulator
  • exempt from registration with a charity regulator
  • your organisation is established in a country where there is no charity regulator

You are excepted if income is £100,000 or less and your charity is in one of the following groups:

  • churches or chapels belonging to some Christian denominations
  • charities that provide premises for some types of school
  • Scout and Guide groups
  • charitable service funds of the armed forces
  • student unions

You are exempt from registering with the relevant regulator because you are:

  • an educational institution such as a university, school or connected institution
  • a museum or gallery, as defined in the Charities Act
  • a housing association registered under the Industrial and Provident Societies Act 1965

Help about: Corporate trustee

A corporate trustee is responsible for looking after the day-to-day administration of the charity. Only a charity managed by a trust company, or trust department of a bank can have a corporate trustee.

Help about: Authorised official

If you are not a corporate trustee, enter the details of the official you have told HMRC is authorised to make this repayment claim.

These details will be the personal information of the authorised official supplied to HMRC when

  • the charity applied to be recognised by HMRC, or
  • when the CASC submitted its registration, or
  • when the details were amended by submitting a change of details form (ChV1).

If the authorised official has changed you must tell HMRC who the new authorised official is by submitting a change of details form (ChV1) at least 30 days before making this claim.
