Who is eligible for no registration with a charity regulator?

You are excepted if income is £100,000 or less and your charity is in one of the following groups:

  • churches or chapels belonging to some Christian denominations
  • charities that provide premises for some types of school
  • Scout and Guide groups
  • charitable service funds of the armed forces
  • student unions

You are exempt from registering with the relevant regulator because you are:

  • an educational institution such as a university, school or connected institution
  • a museum or gallery, as defined in the Charities Act
  • a housing association registered under the Industrial and Provident Societies Act 1965

You can apply directly to HMRC for recognition as a charity for tax purposes, if you are:

  • excepted from registration with a charity regulator
  • exempt from registration with a charity regulator
  • your organisation is established in a country where there is no charity regulator

Help about: Authorised official

If you are not a corporate trustee, enter the details of the official you have told HMRC is authorised to make this repayment claim.

These details will be the personal information of the authorised official supplied to HMRC when

  • the charity applied to be recognised by HMRC, or
  • when the CASC submitted its registration, or
  • when the details were amended by submitting a change of details form (ChV1) (opens in a new tab).

If the authorised official has changed you must tell HMRC who the new authorised official is by submitting a change of details form (ChV1) at least 30 days before making this claim.
